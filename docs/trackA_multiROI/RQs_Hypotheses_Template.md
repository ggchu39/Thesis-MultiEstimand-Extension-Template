# Research questions and hypotheses (template)

This template supports two-wave longitudinal analyses testing coupling between within-person change in a **feature/ROI value** and within-person change in an **outcome**, including robustness across change estimands and optional effect-heterogeneity and descriptive decomposition analyses.

> Use placeholders only (e.g., `[feature]`, `[outcome]`, `[group]`). Do not commit dataset-specific variable names or results.

## Research questions

**RQ1 (primary): Within-person coupling**  
Across pre-specified regions/features, does within-person change in **[feature]** covary with within-person change in **[outcome]** across follow-up?

**RQ2 (secondary): Effect heterogeneity**  
Does the within-person **[feature]–[outcome]** coupling differ by **[group]** (e.g., sex)?

**RQ3 (secondary): Mean change and group differences in change**  
Do **[outcome]** and **[feature]** show mean change over time, and does the amount of change differ by **[group]**?

**RQ4 (secondary): Feature/region specificity and robustness**  
Is coupling stronger in some pre-specified regions/features than others, and is any regional/feature pattern consistent across multiple change estimands?

**RQ5 (secondary / targeted): Statistical decomposition (non-causal)**  
To what extent can change in **[feature]** statistically account for any observed **[group]** difference in **[outcome change]** (descriptive decomposition, not a causal claim)?

## Hypotheses (examples)

**H1 (feature change):** **[feature]** shows average change from Wave 1 to Wave 2 with inter-individual variability.  
**H2 (outcome change):** **[outcome]** shows average change from Wave 1 to Wave 2 with inter-individual variability.  
**H3 (coupling):** Greater decline (or increase) in **[feature]** is associated with greater decline (or increase) in **[outcome]** (directional coupling).  
**H4 (moderation):** The coupling slope differs by **[group]**.  
**H5 (specificity):** Coupling differs across regions/features and may be most evident where measurement stability is higher.  
**H6 (decomposition):** Any **[group]** difference in **[outcome change]** is only weakly (if at all) statistically explained by **[feature change]**.
